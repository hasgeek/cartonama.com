Twitter hashtag chat with Henry Addo 12 September 2012

In case you're new to it, a Twitter hashtag chat is a fun, easy way to talk with people across the world on an interesting topic. All you do is use the same hashtag in your tweets and follow along in your browser or client of choice. We're going to be using  the tag #cartonama for today's chat.

Henry Addo will be joining us in chat today to talk about the state of crowd sourced tools and projects in the mapping and location data ecosystem. Henry is a software developer living in Accra, Ghana. He has worked as a software developer with several IT companies including Suuch Solutions, txtGhana, IDZ Ghana limited. Currently, Henry works at Ushahidi, a non-profit tech company that specializes in developing free and open source software for information collection, visualization and interactive mapping.

He is passionate about open technologies and has been instrumental in the evangelism and advocacy of the use of Free and Open Source Software in Ghana.

Hangout with Bill Morris 12 September 2012

Join Bill Morris and other map data enthusiasts in a Google Hangout tonight at 7:30pm IST, 28 August 2012.

Bill is principal at GeoSprocket LLC, a multifaceted geospatial consulting firm. With over a decade of experience in geographic information science, remote sensing and cartography, he focuses on applying the strength of spatial relationships to real-world challenges in the areas of environmental resource conservation, agricultural management and international development.

Join the Hangout directly and post your questions in live chat or if you don't have a Google+ account you can watch the Hangout live on YouTube.
